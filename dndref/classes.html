Barbarian

Class Information

Hit Points
Hit Dice: 1d12 per Level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 12 + Con modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d12 (or 7) + your Con modifier per Barbarian level after 1st


Proficiencies
Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields
Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution
Skills: Choose two from: Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival


Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
  • A greataxe OR any martial melee weapon
  • Two handaxes OR any simple weapon
  • An explorer's pack
  • Four javelins
Alternatively, you may start with 2d4×10 gp and purchase your starting equipment using these funds.

Class Features

Rage
You may enter a Rage as a bonus action.
While raging, you gain the following benefits if you aren't wearing heavy armor:
  • Advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws
  • Gain a bonus to melee weapon damage as shown on the advancement table
  • Resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage
Your rage lasts 1 minute. It ends early if:
  • You are knocked unconscious
  • Your turn ends and you haven't dealt or received damage
  • You choose to end your rage as a bonus action
Your uses of Rage replenish after a long rest.


Unarmored Defense
While you are not wearing any armor, your Armor Class equals 10 + Dex mod + Con mod. You may use a shield and retain this effect.


Reckless Attack
On your first attack of your turn, you may opt to gain advantage on an attack; however, attacks made against you also gain advantage until your next turn.


Danger Sense
You gain advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects you can't see, such as traps or spells. You may not gain this effect ifyou are blinded, deafened, or incapacitated.


Primal Path
Choose a path to shape the nature of your rage: Path of the Berzerker, or Path of the Totem Warrior. (pp. 49-50 PHB)


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Extra Attack
You may attack twice whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.


Fast Movement
Your speed increases by 10 feet when you aren't wearing heavy armor.


Feral Instinct
Gain advantage on Initiative rolls. Additionally, if you are Surprised (but not incapacitated) at the beginning of combat, you may act normally on your first turn if you enter your Rage as your first action.


Brutal Critical
Roll one additional weapon damage die when determining critical damage on a melee attack. This increases to two dice at 13th level, and three dice at 17th level.


Relentless Rage
If you drop to 0 hit points while raging, and don't die outright, you may make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. On success, drop to 1 hit point instead. Each time you use this feature (after the first), the DC increases by 5. It resets to DC 10 after a long rest.


Persistent Rage
Your rage only ends early if you choose to end it or fall unconscious.


Indomitable Might
If your total for a Strength check is less than your Strength score, you may use your score in place of the check.


Primal Champion
Your Strength and Constitution scores increase by 4. Your maximum for those scores is now 24.

Class Features

Spellcasting
Your spellcasting ability is Charisma.

Your spell save DC is 8 + Proficiency mod + Char mod, and your spell attack modifier is Proficiency bonus + Char mod.

You may cast any Bard spell as a Ritual, so long as it has the Ritual tag.
You may use any musical instrument as a spellcasting focus for your Bard spells.


Bardic Inspiration
You may use a bonus action to give one creature within 60 feet who can hear you, other than yourself, a bonus die.
Once in the next 10 minutes, that creature may roll that die and add it to any ability check, attack roll, or saving throw.
A creature may only have one Bardic Inspiration die at a time.
You may use this feature a number of times equal to your Char mod, and expended uses are refilled after a long rest.


Jack of All Trades
You may add half your proficiency bonus, rounded down, to any ability check you make that doesn't already include your proficiency bonus.


Song of Rest
During a short rest, you may play a soothing song. If you, or any friendly creatures, regain hit points during the rest, they regain an extra die (based on your level).


Bard College
Delve into the advanced techniques of a bard college: the College of Lore or the College of Valor. (pp. 54-55 PHB)


Expertise
Choose two skill proficiencies; your proficiency bonus is doubled for any Ability check that uses either of those proficiencies.


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Font of Inspiration
You may now regain expended uses of Bardic Inspiration after a short or long rest.


Countercharm
Take an action, which lasts until the end of your next turn, to grant you and any friendly creatures within 30 feet (who can hear you) Advantage on saving throws against being Frightened or Charmed. This ends early if you are incapacitated or silenced, or if you voluntarily end it.


Magical Secrets
Choose two spells from any class, including this one. They must be of a level you can cast, or a cantrip. The chosen spells count as Bard spells (cast using Charisma) and are included in the number of Spells Known in the advancement table.


Superior Inspiration
If you have no uses of Bardic Inspiration left when you roll initiative, regain one.

Class Features

Spellcasting
Cleric spells must be prepared by spending at least 1 hour of a long rest in prayer and meditation. Choose a number of spells equal to your Wisdom mod + Cleric level from the Cleric spell list. The spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

Your spellcasting ability is Wisdom.

Your spell save DC is 8 + Proficiency mod + Wis mod, and your spell attack modifier is Proficiency bonus + Wis mod.

You may cast any Cleric spell as a Ritual, so long as it has the Ritual tag.
You may use a holy symbol as a spellcasting focus for your Cleric spells.


Divine Domain
Choose a domain that is associated with your deity. Your choice grants you domain spells, as well as other features and benefits.

Domain spells are always prepared, and they do not count against your total number of spells prepared for a day.
If you gain a spell from your domain that isn't naturally a Cleric spell, it will count as a Cleric spell for you.

(pp. 59-63 PHB)


Channel Divinity
You may channel the Divinity of your deity for two purposes; to Turn Undead, and for an effect determined by your Domain.

Turn Undead: Take an action to present your holy symbol and speak a prayer. Each undead that can see or hear you within 30 feet must make a Wisdom saving throw. Upon failure, it is turned for 1 minute (or until it takes damage.)


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Destroy Undead
If an undead at or below the CR indicated by this feature fails its Turn Undead save, it is instantly destroyed.


Divine Intervention
Beseech your deity for aid. Roll a percentile die; if the result is equal to or lower than your Cleric level, your deity intervenes. The nature of this intervention is up to the DM.

If successful, you can't use this feature again for 7 days; otherwise, you can use it after a long rest.
At 20th level, your call for intervention automatically succeeds.

Class Features

Spellcasting
Druid spells must be prepared by spending at least 1 minute per spell in prayer and meditation. Choose a number of spells equal to your Wisdom mod + Druid level from the Druid spell list. The spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

Your spellcasting ability is Wisdom.

Your spell save DC is 8 + Proficiency mod + Wis mod, and your spell attack modifier is Proficiency bonus + Wis mod.

You may cast any Druid spell as a Ritual, so long as it has the Ritual tag.
You may use a druidic focus symbol as a spellcasting focus for your Druid spells.


Druidic
You know Druidic, the secret language of druids. You may leave, and will automatically spot, hidden messages in Druidic. Those who do not know Druidic may spot the message with a DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check, but can't decipher it without magic.


Wild Shape
You can use your action to magically assume the shape of a beast that you have seen before. You can use this feature twice, with expended uses restored after a short or long rest.

Your druid level determines the beasts you can transform into.
You can stay in a beast shape for a number of hours equal to half your druid level, rounded down. You then revert to your normal form unless you expend another use of Wild Shape.

(pp. 66-67 PHB)


Druid Circle
Choose to associate with either the Circle of the Land or the Circle of the Moon, which grant additional features.

(pp. 68-69 PHB)


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Timeless Body
The primal magic that you wield causes your body to age more slowly. For every 10 years that pass, your body ages only 1 year.


Beast Spells
You may cast many of your druid spells while in Wild Shape. You can perform the Verbal and Somatic components, but are not able to provide Material components.


Archdruid
You may use Wild Shape an unlimited number of times.

Class Features

Fighting Style
You adopt a particular style of fighting. You can't take a Fighting Style option more than once, even if you later get to choose again.

Archery: Gain +2 bonus to attack rolls made with ranged weapons.

Defense: Gain +1 bonus to AC while wearing armor.

Dueling: Gain +2 bonus to damage when using only a single melee weapon in one hand, and no other weapons.

Great Weapon Fighting: Re-roll 1s or 2s on damage when using a Two-Handed or Versatile weapon. You must keep the second roll.

Protection: While wielding a shield, if a creature you can see attacks a target other than you within 5 feet of you, you may use your reaction to impose disadvantage.

Two-Weapon Fighting: You may add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.


Second Wind
On your turn, you can use a bonus action to regain 1d10 + Fighter level hit points.
After using your Second Wind, you must finish a short or long rest before using it again.


Action Surge
You can take an extra action in addition to your normal action and bonus action.
Once you use this feature, you must finish a short or long rest before using it again.


Martial Archetype
Choose an archetype to emulate in your combat styles. Choose from: Champion, Battle Master, or Eldritch Knight.

(pp. 72-75 PHB)


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Extra Attack
You may attack multiple times whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.


Indomitable
You can re-roll a saving throw that you fail. If you do so, you must use the new roll.
You must finish a long rest to use this feature again.

Class Features

Martial Arts
You gain the following benefits if you are unarmed (or wielding only Monk weapons), and are not wearing armor or wielding a shield:
  • You may use Dexterity instead of Strength for attack and damage rolls
  • You may roll your Martial Arts damage (from the table above) in place of the regular damage of your weapon or unarmed strikes
  • When you make an Attack action, you may make an additional unarmed strike as a bonus action, provided you haven't already taken your bonus action this round.


Unarmored Defense
While you are not wearing any armor, and wielding no shield, your Armor Class equals 10 + Dex mod + Wis mod.


Ki
Your training allows you to harness the mystical energy of Ki. You have a number of Ki points determined by your level, which can be spent to fuel special features.
Your expended Ki points are restored after a short or long rest. You must spend at least 30 minutes of the rest meditating to regain your Ki points.
Some Ki features require a saving throw; the DC is: 8 + Proficiency bonus + Wis mod.

You start knowing these three Ki features:
Flurry of Blows: Immediately after taking an Attack action, you can spend 1 Ki point to make two unarmed strikes as a bonus action.
Patient Defense: You can spend 1 Ki point to take the Dodge action as a bonus action on your turn.
Step of the Wind: You can spend 1 Ki point to take the Disengage or Dash action as a bonus action on your turn, and your jump distance is doubled for the turn.


Unarmored Movement
Your speed increases while you are not wearing armor or wielding a shield.
At 9th level, you gain the ability to move along vertical surfaces and across liquids without falling during the move.

Monastic Tradition
Commit yourself to a Monastic Tradition: The Way of the Open Hand, the Way of Shadow, or the Way of the Four Elements.

(pp. 79-81 PHB)


Deflect Missiles
You can use your reaction to catch or deflect a ranged attack made against you. When you do so, the damage you take is reduced by 1d10 + Dex mod + Monk level.

If you reduce the damage to 0, and the missle is small enough to hold in one hand, and you have one hand free, you can catch the missile. If you do so, you may spend 1 Ki point to make a ranged attack with the missle as part of the same reaction. You make this attack with proficiency, regardless of the type of missle.


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Slow Fall
You can use your reaction when you fall to reduce any falling damage taken by 5×Monk level.


Extra Attack
You can attack twice, instead of once, when you take the Attack action on your turn.


Stunning Strike
When you succeed with a melee attack, you can spend 1 Ki point to attempt to stun the target. The target is stunned until the end of your next turn if they fail a Constitution saving throw.


Ki-Empowered Strikes
Your unarmed strikes count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.


Evasion
You are able to better dodge area effects that require a Dexterity saving throw. If you succeed on the throw, you take no damage. If you fail, you only take half damage.


Stillness of Mind
You can use your action to end one effect on yourself that is causing you to be charmed or frightened.


Purity of Body
You become immune to disease and poison.


Tongue of the Sun and Moon
You are able to understand all spoken languages, and any creature capable of understanding a language can understand what you say.


Diamond Soul
You gain proficiency in all saving throws.
In addition, whenever you fail a saving throw, you may spend 1 Ki point to re-roll it, but you must take the second roll.


Timeless Body
You do not suffer the frailty of old age, nor can you be aged magically. You can still die of old age. In addition, you no longer need food or water.


Empty Body
You can use your action and spend 4 Ki points to become invisible for 1 minute.


Perfect Self
When you roll for initiative and have no Ki points remaining, you regain 4 Ki points.

Hit Points at 1st Level: 10 + Con modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d10 (or 6) + your Con modifier per Ranger level after 1st


Proficiencies
Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields
Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity
Skills: Choose three from: Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, Survival

Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
  • Scale mail OR leather armor
  • Two shortswords OR two simple melee weapons
  • A dungeoneer's pack OR an explorer's pack
  • A longbow and a quiver of 20 arrows
Alternatively, you may start with 5d4×10 gp and purchase your starting equipment using these funds.
